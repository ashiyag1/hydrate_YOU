Why water matters

Your body is mostly water — here's why every sip counts.

Brain is water
Your brain is three-quarters water. Even mild dehydration of 1–2% can impair concentration, memory, and mood.

Blood is water
Blood is about 90% water, carrying oxygen and nutrients to every cell in your body. Stay hydrated, stay energised.

💡 Smart hydration tips

Start every morning with a full glass of water before your first coffee or tea — it kickstarts your metabolism.
Drink a glass before each meal — it can reduce unnecessary snacking and aid digestion.
Set a reminder every 90 minutes on your phone so hydration becomes automatic.
Hot weather, exercise, or illness? Add 500–1000 ml to your daily goal to compensate.
Keep a water bottle at your desk, in your bag, and by your bed — visibility equals action.
Herbal teas, coconut water, and water-rich fruits (watermelon, cucumber) count towards your intake too!
